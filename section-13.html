Section 8.1 Rational Expressions

A rational expression is a polynomial divided by a polynomial. Because of this, you still need the skills used in expanding and condensing polynomial expressions, but will also need to take into account that condensing may happen between the numerator and denominator.

Example 8.1.1.

Consider the rational expression

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{(x+2)^2(x-6)(x-8)}{(x+2)(x+4)(x-8)} \end{equation*}

Write it in the most simple way imaginable.

Hint.
Terms attached through addition and subtraction are "besties" and can't be canceled away from each other. Instead, you may cancel common factors between the numerator and denominator
Answer.
\(\dfrac{(x+2)(x-6)}{(x+4)}\) or \(\dfrac{x^2-4x-12}{x+4}\)

Example 8.1.2.

Write the following rational expression in the most simple way imaginable.

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{x^2-3x-4}{x+1} \end{equation*}
Hint.
You will need to factor the numerator
Answer.
\(x-4\)

Example 8.1.3.

Write the following rational expression in the most simple way imaginable.

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{(x-3)(x+2)}{14x^2-14x-84} \end{equation*}
Answer.
\(\dfrac{1}{14}\)

Exercises Exercises

Exercise Group.

Fully simplify each of the following expressions.

1.

\(\dfrac{(x^2-4)(x-4)^2}{(x+2)^2(x+4)}\)

Answer.
\(\dfrac{(x-2)(x-4)^2}{(x+2)(x+4)}\)
2.
\(\dfrac{(x+2)^{1/2}}{(x+2)^{3/2}}\)
Answer.
\(\dfrac{1}{(x+2)^{1/2}}\)
3.
\(\dfrac{(x-7)}{(x-7)^{-1}}\)
Answer.
\((x-7)^2\)
