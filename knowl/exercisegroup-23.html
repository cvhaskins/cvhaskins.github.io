Q: Find the average rate of change of \(f(x)\) between \(x=-8\) and \(x=4\text{,}\) where \(f(x)=x^2+2\text{.}\)

A:

\begin{equation*} (-8)^2+2=66 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} (4)^2+2=18 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{-8-4}{66-18}=\dfrac{-12}{48}=-\dfrac{1}{4} \end{equation*}

When you plug in the correct numbers and use the formula, \(\dfrac{f(x_1)-f(x_1)}{f(x_2)-f(x_2)}\text{,}\) you get \(-\dfrac{1}{4}\text{.}\)

Q: If \(f(x)=\dfrac{3-5x}{3x}\) and \(g(x)=\dfrac{10}{3x+5}\text{,}\) then find and simplify \((f+g)(x)\text{.}\)

A:

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{3-5x}{3x}+\dfrac{10}{3x+5}=\dfrac{-7+5x}{6x+5} \end{equation*}

Q: Find the equation of the line which is parallel to the line \(8x+2y+6=0\) and that passes through the point \((-3,5)\)

A:

\begin{equation*} y=4x+3 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} y-5 = 4(x+3) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} y-5 = 4x+3 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} y = 4x+8 \end{equation*}

Q: \(f(x) = 16x^4+kx^2+6x+10\) has a zero at \(x = \dfrac{1}{2}\text{.}\) Find the value of \(k\text{.}\)

A:

\begin{equation*} 16\left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^4+k\left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^2+6\left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)+10 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 16\left(\dfrac{1}{16}\right)=1 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 1+k\dfrac{1}{4}+3+10 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 1+k\dfrac{1}{4}+13 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} k\dfrac{1}{4}+12 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} k=48 \end{equation*}
