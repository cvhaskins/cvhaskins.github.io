Exercise 1.

Q: Find the average rate of change of \(f(x)\) between \(x=-8\) and \(x=4\text{,}\) where \(f(x)=x^2+2\text{.}\)

A:

\begin{equation*} (-8)^2+2=66 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} (4)^2+2=18 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{-8-4}{66-18}=\dfrac{-12}{48}=-\dfrac{1}{4} \end{equation*}

When you plug in the correct numbers and use the formula, \(\dfrac{f(x_1)-f(x_1)}{f(x_2)-f(x_2)}\text{,}\) you get \(-\dfrac{1}{4}\text{.}\)
