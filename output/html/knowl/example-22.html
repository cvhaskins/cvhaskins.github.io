Example 6.0.4.

We don't know things about trigonometry, but we can use the idea of substituting symbols in for scary looking things to reduce a trigonometric expression. Here are some rules:

\begin{equation*} \sec\theta = \dfrac{1}{\cos\theta} \hspace{.5in} \csc\theta = \dfrac{1}{\sin\theta} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \tan\theta = \dfrac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta} \hspace{.5in} \cot\theta = \dfrac{\cos\theta}{\sin\theta} \end{equation*}

Use these to reduce:

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{\csc\theta\cos\theta+\cot\theta}{\sec\theta\cot\theta} \end{equation*}
